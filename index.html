Discover Frankfurt

Welcome to Frankfurt, a dynamic and international city in the heart of Germany.

A Brief History of Frankfurt

Frankfurt am Main, often simply referred to as Frankfurt, has a rich history that dates back to Roman times. The city's strategic location along the River Main made it a vital center for trade and commerce. By the Middle Ages, Frankfurt had become a significant city, hosting important fairs and markets that attracted merchants from across Europe.

In 1356, Frankfurt was designated as the site for the election of Holy Roman Emperors, a tradition that continued for several centuries. The city's importance as a trade and financial center grew, and it became one of the most influential cities in the Holy Roman Empire. During World War II, Frankfurt suffered extensive damage from Allied bombing raids, leading to the destruction of much of its historic architecture.

In the post-war period, Frankfurt underwent a remarkable transformation. The city rebuilt itself and emerged as a modern metropolis. Today, Frankfurt stands as a global financial hub, home to the European Central Bank, the Frankfurt Stock Exchange, and numerous multinational companies. Its blend of historic charm and cutting-edge development makes it a unique destination for visitors.

Culture and Attractions

Frankfurt is renowned for its cultural diversity and vibrant arts scene. The city boasts numerous museums, theaters, and galleries, offering a rich array of cultural experiences. The Städel Museum, one of Germany's most important art museums, houses an impressive collection of European art from the Middle Ages to the present. The Alte Oper, a historic opera house, hosts a variety of performances, from classical concerts to contemporary theater.

The city's cultural offerings extend beyond traditional venues. Frankfurt's streets are alive with festivals, markets, and events throughout the year. The annual Frankfurt Book Fair, the world's largest trade fair for books, attracts authors, publishers, and literary enthusiasts from around the globe. Additionally, the city's culinary scene is a testament to its multicultural population, with an array of restaurants offering both local and international cuisine.

Visitors can enjoy a stroll along the picturesque River Main, explore the historic Römer district with its charming half-timbered houses, or relax in one of Frankfurt's many green spaces, such as the Palmengarten, a beautiful botanical garden. The city's lively atmosphere and diverse attractions make it a must-visit destination for culture lovers.

Modern Frankfurt

Frankfurt today is a thriving international city known for its modern architecture, financial prowess, and technological innovation. The city's skyline, dominated by towering skyscrapers, is a testament to its role as a leading financial center. Notable buildings include the Commerzbank Tower, the Messeturm, and the Main Tower, which offers an observation deck with stunning views of the city.

Frankfurt is also a hub for education and research, home to prestigious institutions such as Goethe University and the Frankfurt School of Finance & Management. The city's commitment to sustainability and innovation is evident in initiatives like the Frankfurt Green City project, which aims to promote environmental sustainability through urban planning and development.

The city is well-connected, with one of the world's busiest airports, Frankfurt Airport, serving as a major gateway to Europe and beyond. Public transportation within the city is efficient and reliable, making it easy for residents and visitors to navigate. From its bustling financial district to its serene parks and vibrant cultural scene, modern Frankfurt offers a dynamic and enriching experience for all who visit.
